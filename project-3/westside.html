Westside

The Westside of Los Angeles encompasses coastal neighborhoods like Santa Monica, commercial hubs, and cultural landmarks. The region faces unique heat challenges driven by dense urbanization and extensive paved surfaces. While wealthier than many LA areas, pockets of vulnerability still exist near older multi-family housing and transit corridors.

Overview — Westside

Average Los Angeles High Temperature:
• August (Hottest Month): 85° F
• December (Coldest Month): 67° F

Heat Island Index: Coastal LA remains fairly cool, but is still vulnerable to high summer heat.

Population: ~600,000 residents
Racial/Ethnic Composition:
• ~40% White
• ~30% Latinx
• ~20% Asian
• ~5% Black
• ~5% Mixed/Other

Heat Vulnerability Indicators:
• Older adult households
• Elevated exposure near industrial corridors and transit hubs

Heat Inequity Indicators

Tree Canopy Coverage: ~21–23% (County avg ~18%)
Median Household Income: ~$120,000
LA County Median Household Income: ~$88,000
Below 100% Poverty Threshold: 7%

Heat Exposure:
• Average Energy Burden: 1.8% of income
• Mostly walkable neighborhoods
• Many bus stops partially shaded

Historical Trends — Westside

Temperature Change:
• +2–3°F rise since 1950
• Coastal breezes mitigate some urban heat

Urban Development:
• Postwar suburban expansion and commercial development
• Freeway construction increased paved areas
• Beachfront and inland neighborhoods densified

Demographic Change:
• Long history of affluent White populations
• Increasing diversity with Latinx and Asian communities
• Gentrification and rising rents in Venice, Mar Vista

Modern Disparity:
• Some lower-income pockets overlooked in shade and cooling investments
• Limited public infrastructure to mitigate heat in older areas

Solutions & Interventions

Green Infrastructure:
• Environmental advocacy programs in Venice and Westwood
• Shade structures at key transit stops

Community Solutions:
• Local climate and tree planting programs
• Advocacy for shade equity and heat mitigation

Government Policies:
• LA County Heat Action Plan coverage includes Westside
• Incentives for cool roofs and heat-resistant materials
