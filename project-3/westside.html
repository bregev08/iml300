Westside

The Westside of Los Angeles spans from Santa Monica to Westwood, encompassing coastal neighborhoods, commercial hubs, and cultural landmarks. The region faces unique heat challenges driven by dense urbanization, limited inland greenery, and extensive paved surfaces. While wealthier than many LA areas, pockets of vulnerability still exist near older multi-family housing and transit corridors.

Overview — Westside

Average Annual Temperature: ~70°F
Extreme Heat Days: 25–35 days/year above 95°F
Heat Island Index: +4–6°F warmer than coastal LA

Population: ~600,000 residents
Racial/Ethnic Composition:
• ~40% White
• ~30% Latinx
• ~20% Asian
• ~5% Black
• ~5% Mixed/Other

Heat Vulnerability Indicators:
• ~15% adults with heat-sensitive conditions
• Older adult households concentrated in Brentwood & Palms
• High transit exposure along major corridors

Heat Inequity Indicators

Tree Canopy Coverage: ~20–25% (County avg ~21%)
Lowest canopy areas: Century City, Palms, Venice

Median Household Income: ~$120,000 (lower-income pockets ~55k)
Poverty Rate: ~8–15% depending on neighborhood

Cooling Centers: ~10 locations across the Westside
Homes Without AC: ~10–15%
Utility Cost Burden: ~5–8% in lower-income areas

Transportation Exposure:
• Walkability scores 70–85
• Many bus stops partially shaded
• Transit riders exposed to 8–12°F higher wait-time temps

Historical Trends — Westside

Temperature Change:
• +2–3°F average rise since 1950
• Extreme heat days increased steadily
• Coastal breezes mitigate some urban heat

Urban Development:
• Postwar suburban expansion and commercial development
• Freeway construction increased paved areas
• Beachfront and inland neighborhoods densified

Demographic Change:
• Long history of affluent White populations
• Increasing diversity with Latinx and Asian communities
• Gentrification and rising rents in Venice, Mar Vista

Environmental Justice History:
• Some lower-income pockets overlooked in shade and cooling investments
• Limited public infrastructure to mitigate heat in older multifamily areas

Solutions & Interventions

Green Infrastructure:
• Tree planting programs in Venice, Westwood, and Palms
• Cool pavement pilots along heavily trafficked streets
• Shade structures at key transit stops

Community Solutions:
• Neighborhood cooling hubs
• Local youth-led climate and tree planting programs
• Advocacy for shade equity and heat mitigation

Government Policies:
• LA County Heat Action Plan coverage includes Westside
• Investment in “shade deserts” and public spaces
• Incentives for cool roofs and heat-resistant materials

Tech & Design Interventions:
• Smart heat sensors in schools and rec centers
• Reflective roofing and insulation upgrades
• Heat-resistant materials in new residential and commercial development
