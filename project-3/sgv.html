San Gabriel Valley

The San Gabriel Valley is a diverse and rapidly urbanizing region east of Los Angeles, featuring a mix of historic neighborhoods, suburban sprawl, and industrial corridors. Structural inequities in infrastructure and planning make some areas particularly vulnerable to rising temperatures and climate hazards.

Overview — San Gabriel Valley

Average Los Angeles High Temperature:
• August (Hottest Month): 85° F
• December (Coldest Month): 67° F

Heat Island Index: +6–8°F warmer than coastal LA

Population: ~1.5 million residents
Racial/Ethnic Composition:
• ~45% Latinx
• ~30% Asian
• ~15% White
• ~5% Black
• ~5% Mixed/Other

Heat Vulnerability Indicators:
• High rates of cardiovascular and respiratory conditions
• Concentrated low-income housing and older adult populations
• Elevated exposure near highways and industrial areas

Heat Inequity Indicators

Tree Canopy Coverage: ~14–16% (County avg. 18%)
Median Household Income: ~$75,000
LA County Median Household Income: ~$88,000
Below 100% Poverty Threshold: 15%

Heat Exposure:
• Average Energy Burden: 1.8% of income
• Mix of walkable and un-walkable neighborhoods
• Bus and metro riders can be exposed to heat on long waits

Historical Trends — San Gabriel Valley

Temperature Change:
• +3.5°F rise since 1950

Urban Development:
• Rapid postwar suburban expansion
• Industrial corridors and freeways added heat-absorbing surfaces
• Loss of orchards and agricultural land in the valley

Demographic Change:
• Post-1950s white suburbanization boom
• Increasing Latinx and Asian immigrant populations after 1980
• Gentrification pressures in Pasadena & Pasadena foothills

Modern Disparity:
• Policies historically neglected tree canopy & shade without public pressure

Solutions & Interventions

Green Infrastructure:
• Tree planting initiatives by local organizations
• Shade structures added to key transit stops

Community Solutions:
• Neighborhood mutual-aid networks
• Youth climate programs

Government Policies:
• San Gabriel Valley included in LA County Heat Action Plan
